Overall architecture

An ecosystem structured across three layers of value.

D2F articulates a single vision through three complementary layers: the production of analysis, the assurance of compliance, and the exploration of operational orchestration models at the European level.

Think

The research, analytical and intellectual production unit dedicated to the governance architecture of European e-invoicing systems.

  • conceptual reading frameworks
  • comparative analysis of national architectures
  • interpretation of governance and compliance logic

Verify

D2F Compliant

The assurance, audit and evaluation layer designed to assess the robustness of compliance arrangements, traceability, and evidentiary continuity.

  • governance audit
  • traceability assessment
  • regulatory alignment review

Implement

D2Finity

The operational layer focused on exploring structures capable of orchestrating compliance requirements across multi-jurisdictional environments.

  • orchestration logic

  • articulation between rules, flows, and evidence

Method

A structured analytical approach.

The work developed within D2F Normative Lab is based on an analytical method combining regulatory reading, architectural comparison, and interpretation of governance logic across European e-invoicing systems.

Analytical pillars

  • architecture of validation authority
  • distribution of compliance responsibility
  • traceability and evidentiary continuity
  • interaction between national infrastructures

Research themes

The main questions explored by the Lab.

Validation Authority

Where validation authority sits within European e-invoicing architectures, and how that location reshapes compliance governance.

Compliance Orchestration

How organisations preserve regulatory coherence across fragmented national systems.

Digital VAT Futures

How initiatives such as ViDA transform reporting obligations, governance models, and operational infrastructures.

Interpretation principle

This is not a juxtaposition of offers, but a coherent architecture.

The overall logic should not be presented as a simple stack of services. It follows a clear order: understand, assess, and then organise the conditions of execution.

How the three layers connect

D2F Normative Lab analyses the governance architectures that structure European e-invoicing systems. This work informs the audit, traceability, and assurance approaches developed by D2F Compliant. At the operational level, D2Finity explores the conditions under which these architectures may be orchestrated coherently across multiple jurisdictions.

Positioning

A continuum between normative analysis, assurance, and operational coherence.

The strength of the ecosystem lies in the continuity between its three layers. Analysis is not isolated from audit; audit is not detached from implementation realities. Each layer reinforces the next without blurring its role.

What this architecture enables

  • structuring a reference narrative on European e-invoicing
  • strengthening the credibility of audit and compliance approaches
  • preparing an orchestration logic at multi-country level
  • avoiding confusion between reflection, assurance, and execution
